A personal site gets better when it stops trying to look like a product.

The thing I like about Derek Sivers' blog is that it does not beg for
attention. It trusts the reader. The page is plain, but not careless. The
links are obvious. The text has room to breathe. Nothing competes with the
sentence you are reading now.

That is a useful reminder for a small blog.

When a site is mostly writing, the design job is not to decorate it. The job is
to make reading feel calm, direct, and a little inevitable.

> Good design for an essay is often just enough structure to keep you reading.

So this version stays narrow, warm, and deliberate. It uses a paper-tinted
background instead of hard white, keeps the archive list almost severe, and
lets the typography carry most of the personality.

I want the site to feel like a page someone maintains by hand.
